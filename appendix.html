Brief A · Investment Committee · April 2026

Atlas · Airspace Defense (integrated air defense) & Counter-UAS Market Primer

Atlas · Airspace Defense is one module within COMMAND — DxD’s heterogeneous command & control platform. This brief focuses on the counter-UAS capability and its market.

Counter-UAS is no longer a fringe defense category — it is now a federally-coordinated procurement priority. The DoD’s Joint Counter-small Unmanned Aircraft Systems Office (JCO), established in 2020 with the Army as executive agent, was created precisely to consolidate and prioritize cUAS acquisition across the services. The DoD’s own combatant-command after-action reporting from sustained Red Sea operations in 2023–2024 (Operation Prosperity Guardian / Operation Poseidon Archer) repeatedly described U.S. Navy SM-2 / SM-6 class missiles — with unit costs running roughly $1M–$4M apiece — being expended against Iranian Shahed-136 / similar one-way attack drones with reported unit costs in the ~$20K range. That cost-exchange is the procurement thesis driving every kinetic-cUAS RFP across DoD, DHS, and allied MoDs in 2026.

U.S. industrial base capacity for kinetic interceptors today is widely characterized in public testimony as production-constrained — demand outstrips deliveries at every tier — against threat profiles informed by the Ukraine and Red Sea theaters where attritional drone use has become routine.

Brief B · 26 partners · 7 MOUs signed

Partnership Ecosystem

DxD’s moat is a stack of partners that each compress one of the four frictions blocking competitors: regulatory, operational, deployment, and integration.

The cUAS market has four structural frictions that delay every new entrant by 18–36 months: (1) regulatory waivers (FAA Part 107.31 / Part 108 / Section 333), (2) operational credentialing (CJIS, security-clearance pipelines, ATAK federations), (3) deployment access (master service agreements with site owners, NDAs, insurance carve-outs), and (4) integration (hardware/software certification across radar, RF, EO/IR, and effector vendors). DxD’s 26-partner stack compresses each one.

Of those 26 partners, 7 have signed MOUs on file (data room, Tab 4) and 3 represent exclusive commercial rights — the most material of which is Orcrist, where DxD holds the exclusive global commercial distribution license through the SPV structure described in the financials.
